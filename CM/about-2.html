What qualities are 3D-printers capable of expressing? What intentions might the machine have, that are overlooked with a focus on accuracy and efficiency? And how do these shape the dialogue between the maker and the machine? Our print-shop offers a unique experience, exploring the machine intentionality of 3D printers.

We invite experienced makers based in Canada to engage with our printers: the breezy printer, the parcel printer, the dwindling printer and the sparse printer. Select the printer you want to work with and submit your 3D-model. The printer of choice either redirects, reshapes, reduces or redistributes. Our printshop employees will handle your files with care, package and ship your completed print to you. Upon receiving your print, you are invited to resubmit a file to print with the same intentional machine.

We are a group of design researchers from the Interactive Futures Lab (Faculty of Design, OCAD) and the Everyday Design Studio (SIAT, SFU). This print service is part of a research project that investigates relations between makers and machines, particularly 3D printers. Through an explorative design process of reconfiguring parameters on a Prusa printer (both on the level of g-code/m-code and through the slicer), we arrived at the four different printers we present here. We are excited to see how other makers will engage with them!

We are happy to process your print at one of our two locations.
